Let's Encode!

Crowd-encoding the world's music scores, one note at a time.

Let's Encode! is a citizen-science project that helps anyone turn sheet music into rich, machine-readable encodings in the MEI format — no specialist training required.

About the project

Making music readable by machines

Digital musical scores are essential tools in both performance and musicology. But standard PDFs and scanned sheet music can't easily be processed by computers: musicians instantly grasp their meaning, yet the musical semantics stay hidden from machines.

Let's Encode! builds infrastructure around mei-friend, a free, browser-based music encoding editor, to enable crowd-based encoding and validation of musical scores. This lets interested people take part in digitising music — without extensive technical knowledge or administrative barriers — and helps build a shared, openly reusable library of encoded music.

We've tried to remove every obstacle between you and the music you want to encode:

  • Free and open source — no fees; our software is openly licensed, and you choose how your encodings are shared, with open, reuse-friendly licences recommended.
  • Nothing to install — our tools run entirely in your browser, on whatever device you already have.
  • You're in charge — campaigns are user-directed, so you can start and manage your own campaign, not just take part in someone else's — all from your browser.

What we're working towards

Project goals

We investigate ways to make semantic music-encoding technologies accessible to diverse communities. Early collaborations include:

  • Chamber orchestras encoding performance scores of public-domain works, reducing licensing fees and building reusable editions.
  • Music societies preserving cultural heritage — lute-music archives, klezmer collections, and the work of underrepresented composers.
  • RISM users creating melodic incipits for the Répertoire International des Sources Musicales to enrich and improve its collections.
